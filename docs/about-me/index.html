About Me#

My name is Tadeo Donegana Braunschweig, i was born and raised in a little town (~8000 habts.) near the center of the state of Buenos Aires in Argentina.

Since i was a child i was always interested in Technology and Science, that interest led me to create a science blog called ‘Todo Ciencia’ back in 2014 with only 13 years old. That was my first approach to the world of programming, despite that the blog was built using Blogger.

A few years later after the launch of Todo Ciencia i decided to ask my parents for an Android Development Course i saw on a Youtube Ad. After much insistence, my parents decided to buy that course, and from that moment to these days i have been really interested in programming learning a lot of technologies and developing some cool projects.

During my High School years i learned a lot of new things and i participated in several Science Fairs and Robotics Competitions as a part of an STEM club i started during those years. There are a few media reports about those activities, be sure to check them out if you are interested:

  • Se conocieron los ganadores del concurso “Pequeños Inventores” (September 2015).
  • Todos podemos hacer un robot (November 2018)
  • “Esperanza I”, la cara olavarriense en los Juegos Bonaerenses 2019 (July 2019)

Also in 2018 i decided to create a Youtube Channel to share some of the projects i was building using Arduino, i started uploading frequently and the channel started to grow. Nowadays that Youtube Channel called ‘El Taller De TD’ has more than 13,000 suscribers and about 500,000 views. I still uploading content about programming, electronics and algorithms often, also Hackaday wrote about one of my projects, you can read the article here:

  • This Arduino feeds the dog (March 2019).

During the winter break of my last year of High School i created one of my most important projects to date. All started when i was watching a morning TV Show and i heard about the rise of poverty in my country and the effects of the ruthless winter of 2020, at that moment i realized that i should do something to help, so i coded Ayumapp in just two weeks during my break from High School.

Ayumapp was a free mobile application with the aim to help homeless people from all around the globe. The main idea of the application is that users can add locations where they find cases of homelessness on the map, so that other users of the application can go to these places to help people. Users also can also add comments about whether there are any specific needs, for example whether there are children or animals.

Ayumapp has more than 10,000 downloads and it was used from all around the globe. Nowadays the app development is discontinued because of my university studies, but i want to continue developing it in a near future.

Also i have given several talks in events like Arduino Day Quilmes 2019 Argentina, you can find those in the media section of this post.

After Ayumapp success, in 2020, I started studying Systems Engineering at the Universidad Nacional del Centro de la Provincia de Buenos Aires (UNICEN). During this three years i have also participated into some academic projects, one of those is this open source simple device for CO2 measurement, where i worked with Dr. Elias Todorovich and other collaborators. You can find more info about the project here.

At the moment i am a full time student and i like to develop some stuff from time to time, from Youtube videos to some cool software and hardware projects. I am also very interested in the Machine Learning and Data Science fields and also i really enjoy making coffee and playing video games 😃.
